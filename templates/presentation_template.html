Why These Inferential Methods?

Plot 6 — Log-linear regression

Biological rationale: epidemic growth follows N(t) = N₀ · ert. Log-transforming linearises → OLS is appropriate.

Estimate: growth rate r [day⁻¹] and doubling time T₂ = ln 2 / r per wave.

Limitation: only the growth phase (~first 40 % of wave days); peak and decline are not exponential.

Assumptions: homogeneous mixing, no saturation, no large reporting changes within the window.

Plot 7 — Holt-Winters forecast

Preferred over ARIMA because:

  • COVID waves are non-stationary — ARIMA differencing discards the trend signal of interest
  • Short training windows (~10 weeks) — fewer hyper-parameters, less risk of overfitting
  • 90 % CI = residual σ × √k (k = forecast horizon step)

Two test periods: Wave 2 (Oct–Dec 2020) and Omicron onset (Jan–Mar 2022) — different growth regimes.

Conclusions

  • Five distinct waves driven by variant succession (Wild-type → Alpha → Delta → Omicron); exponential growth rates 0.05 – 0.14 day⁻¹.
  • Vaccination → lower mortality: CFR fell from ~3.5 % (Wave 1, limited testing) to <0.2 % after booster rollout.
  • Omicron (Wave 5): highest incidence ever recorded, yet lowest CFR — consistent with partial immune escape but reduced severity.
  • Excess mortality peaked in Wave 2 winter; largely absent during Omicron, confirming vaccination impact.
  • Log-linear regression confirms exponential growth phase in all identified growth periods; Holt-Winters forecasts stayed within 90 % CI in both test periods.
  • Germany vs Poland: broadly synchronous epidemic curves; Germany maintained lower peak death rates throughout.
